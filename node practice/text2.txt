Agile development is an iterative approach to software development that emphasizes delivering a working product quickly and frequently. It involves close collaboration between the development team and the customer to ensure that the product meets their needs and expectations. 
Agile development is especially beneficial for projects that are complex or have uncertain requirements. It focuses on: 
Creating working software quickly
Collaborating with customers frequently
Being able to adapt to changes easily
Delivering small but consumable increments of work
Agile development involves: 
Self-organized, cross-functional teams
Frequently analyzing circumstances and user needs to adapt projects
Scrum teams constantly improving quality in sprints with short-term deliverables
Requirements and solutions evolving through collaboration between self-organizing cross-functional teams
The four typical Agile team roles include: 
Product Owner
Team Lead or Scrum master
Development team members
Stakeholders
